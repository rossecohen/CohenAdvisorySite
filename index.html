What I Do

Big companies are built to protect what already works, which is what makes launches from inside one so hard. Anything new needs to survive the CFO's math, the risk committee, compliance review, technology and security assessments, and a sales force that decides what it will and won't sell. Most launches fail long before they ever see the market.

I lead new product, platform, and channel launches that need to go right the first time. I've done it as a Fortune 100 P&L owner, as a COO building platforms for a 12,000-person field force, and as a founder betting my own money. I bring the judgment to make the calls data can't make for you, the credibility to win over the people whose job is to say no, and the accountability to stay until the results show up.

Engagements

There are two ways to work together, and most clients start with the first.

The Launch Assessment

You're weighing a new product, platform, or channel and need a straight answer before real money moves. In two to three weeks, fixed fee, I pressure-test the opportunity: market sizing, unit economics, competitive dynamics, demand smoke tests, key challenges, and risks identified. It ends with a go / no-go recommendation and the evidence behind it. If the answer is no, you'll find out in weeks, not quarters.

The Launch Blueprint

The opportunity is real. Now you need everything required to get to yes and deliver on it. The Blueprint is the approval package and the launch plan: business case, financial model, risk and compliance framework, committee-ready materials, and a sequenced execution plan with workstreams, owners, timelines, and early-warning metrics. All built to survive the CFO, the chief risk officer, and the board.

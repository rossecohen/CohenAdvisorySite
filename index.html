Case Studies
Insurance

Building a modern operating infrastructure for a 12,000-person field organization that had none.

Result

The CRM was adopted by 90% of active agents. The digital selling platform increased sales close rates by 30% for new agents.

The Situation

The company's field organization had been running on the same digital infrastructure for twenty years: tools that were past end of life, poorly adopted, and increasingly irrelevant to how modern agents wanted to work. Competitors were investing heavily in agent technology. Newer, more digitally sophisticated players were starting to erode the company's relevance with prospective agents and clients alike. The risk was becoming existential. An organization that can't attract and equip a productive field force doesn't grow. But the scale of the challenge was daunting: 12,000 agents and financial advisors across a highly distributed, relationship-driven organization, each with their own way of working, resistant to change, and skeptical of technology that had let them down before. Building the infrastructure was only half the problem. Getting a field force of that size to actually use it was the other half.

What I Did

The scale of the challenge required building two things simultaneously: the technology and the operating model to drive adoption of it. Built the team and established a product operating model that integrated delivery, training, incentives, and frontline workflows so adoption was designed into every release. Then worked through the ecosystem systematically: Sales Central to establish the data foundation, GuideMe to transform the client-facing selling experience, Dashboard for performance visibility, Dialogue and Coaching 360 for skill development at scale, and Info Central to solve the communication gap. Each platform required its own product and adoption strategies. The through line was consistent: build it right, train it deeply, motivate the behavior, measure relentlessly.

Financial Services

Launching a net-new credit product in the aftermath of the great recession.

Result

Scaled to over one million customers, making the Secured Card the company's highest volume online credit product.

The Situation

The great recession had fundamentally changed the consumer credit landscape overnight. Millions of Americans who had previously qualified for unsecured credit were suddenly being declined, and the company was turning away enormous volumes of prospects with nowhere to send them. The opportunity was obvious: build a product for this population before competitors did. But the urgency created its own pressure. Move too slowly and the window closes. Move too fast without the right infrastructure and you take on risk you can't manage. The Secured Card didn't exist yet. Neither did the acquisition strategy, the underwriting model, or the operational infrastructure to serve this customer segment. Everything had to be built simultaneously, under competitive pressure, with real money on the line.

What I Did

Came in as general manager and took full P&L ownership of a business that didn't exist yet. The first challenge was definitional: figuring out what product would work for this population, at what price point, with what credit parameters, through what acquisition channels. Built the analytics infrastructure to test and learn quickly. Stood up the cross-functional team, defined the acquisition strategy, and built the underwriting model. Made the calls on channel mix, pricing, and credit policy that determined whether the economics worked.

Growth Infrastructure

Building the distribution and growth capabilities a fast-scaling business had outgrown its ability to manage.

Result

Doubled monthly sales volume and supported growth from $50M to $120M+ in ARR under sustained competitive pressure.

The Situation

The company had scaled fast enough that its operating infrastructure couldn't keep pace. Growth had plateaued and the leadership team was making decisions on instinct because the analytics foundation to answer basic questions about channel performance, lead conversion, and sales productivity didn't exist. Meanwhile competitive pressure wasn't letting up. The CEO needed capabilities the business had never had — and needed someone who could build them without slowing the business down in the process.

What I Did

Built the analytics foundation that made diagnosis possible: a measurement system that could tell us which channels were working, where leads were converting and where they were dying, and why. From there, rebuilt the customer acquisition and conversion system from the ground up — overhauling channel mix, replacing gut-feel decision making with a rigorous experimentation agenda, and working with the sales organization to improve conversion using real-time analytics. All while the business was still running at full speed.
